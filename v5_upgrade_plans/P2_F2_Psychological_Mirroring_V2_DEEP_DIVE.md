## 2. Deep Research Insights

### 2.1 Textual Micro-Expression NLP
The system utilizes a custom Transformer-based model (NIM-optimized) to detect **Textual Disfluencies** that signal cognitive load and emotional friction:
*   **The "Um" vs. "Uh" Delay:** The CRE monitors the duration and frequency of filled pauses. An "Um" (longer delay) precedes complex lexical retrieval, signaling a deep-seated value conflict or hesitation. An "Uh" (shorter) signals simple sentence planning.
*   **Lexical Fillers (Editing Terms):** Phrases like *"Actually," "Well,"* or *"I mean"* are flagged as 'Editing Markers' where the seller is revising their intent in real-time.
*   **False Starts:** Mid-sentence restarts signal high cortisol levels or a shift from 'Truth-Telling' to 'Strategic Obfuscation'.

### 2.2 Linguistic Fingerprinting for CRE
Archetypes are now defined by their **Syntactic Fingerprint**:
*   **The Legacy Steward (Inheritor):** High frequency of plural pronouns (*"We," "The family"*) and past-tense sentimentality. Syntax is passive, reflecting a lack of agency.
*   **The Burned-Out Operator (Tired Landlord):** High density of 'Burden Lexicon' (*"Headache," "Drain," "Repairs"*) and passive voice regarding asset management, shifting to active voice when discussing 'Exit' or 'Relief'.
*   **The High-Velocity Arbitrageur (Institutional):** Future-oriented, high-velocity syntax. Low lexical diversity (TTR) focusing purely on performance metrics (*"IRR," "Cap Ex," "Basis"*).

### 2.3 FBI Rapport-Building Algorithms (BCSM)
The system enforces the **Behavioral Change Stairway Model (BCSM)**:
1.  **Active Listening (Mirroring):** CRE suggests "Last-3-Word" repeats to force expansion.
2.  **Tactical Empathy (Labeling):** Identifying and naming the 'Pain Column' (e.g., *"It sounds like you're worried about the legacy of your father's building"*).
3.  **Accusation Audit:** Proactive neutralisation of seller fears (e.g., *"You probably think we're just another group looking to re-trade you at the finish line"*).

## 4. Discourse Alignment Scoring (DAS)

The DAS is a mathematical model that measures **Linguistic Style Matching (LSM)** between the Principal and Counterparty.

**Formula:**
`DAS = Σ |Function_Word_Ratio_User - Function_Word_Ratio_Seller|`
*Function words include: Personal pronouns, Impersonal pronouns, Articles, Prepositions, Auxiliary verbs, Conjunctions, Adverbs, Negations.*

*   **Score > 0.8 (Synchrony):** High Rapport. Trigger **'The Push'** (Ask for the close).
*   **Score 0.5 - 0.7 (Alignment):** Steady State. Continue **'Tactical Empathy'**.
*   **Score < 0.4 (Friction):** High Resistance. Trigger **'Strategic Deference'**.

Knowing when to play **'Low-D'** (Deference) vs. **'High-D'** (Dominance) is governed by the **DAS Resistance Loop**:

### 5.1 The Deference Trigger (Low-D Play)

*   **Action:** System forces **'Presumptive Leadership'**.
*   **Tactics:** Transition from empathy to **'Certainty-Based Language'**.

The most critical moment in a deal is the 'Pivot' from emotional rapport to mathematical reality.

### 6.1 The Transition Signal (The "Green Light")

1.  **Drop in "Um" Frequency:** Seller has finished the 'Planning/Hesitation' phase.

3.  **Linguistic Destination Phrases:** Mentions of the future *after* the sale (*"Once we move to Florida," "After the taxes are paid"*).
